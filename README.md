# Living vs. Non-Living Sorter

A free, hands-on science activity for **Grades 1–3**. Students sort eight
things — a frog, a sunflower, a tree, a child, a rock, a teddy bear, a
cloud, and a robot — into **Living** and **Non-Living** bins.

Three of the items are deliberately "tricky" (teddy bear, cloud, robot) to
surface the most common misconception at this age: *that moving or looking
alive means being alive.* Wrong guesses get a gentle, specific hint and can
be re-sorted, so mistakes become part of the learning.

## Try it

It's a single `index.html` file. Open it in any modern browser — no install,
no internet connection required after the page loads.

**Live version (GitHub Pages):**
Enable Pages in your repo settings (Settings → Pages → deploy from the

## Features

- **Drag and drop** items into either bin.
- **Fully keyboard accessible** — Tab to a thing, press Enter or Space to
  grab it, Tab to a bin, press Enter to drop. Press Escape to let go.
- **Screen-reader friendly** — every move and the win are announced.
- **Forgiving scoring** — misplaced items show a dashed orange outline and
  can be moved again; correct ones lock in with a green check.
- **Works offline** and adapts to light or dark mode.
- **Original artwork** — all icons are hand-built SVG, no third-party assets.

## How to use it in class

- Project it for a whole-class warm-up, or let students explore on tablets.
- Ask them to predict whether the robot and cloud are alive *before* sorting.
- After sorting, discuss the test for life: *Does it grow? Does it need food
  or water? Can it have young?*
